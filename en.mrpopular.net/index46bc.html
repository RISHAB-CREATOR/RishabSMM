What IMDb is

IMDb is an Internet movie database. It would take too many words to describe the scope and importance of this project. But in short, IMDb is an incarnation of the entire global film industry in the virtual world.

The service provides all the known information about almost every film on the planet. There is everything: from actors, directors, and box office stats up to every article ever written about it. Also, you can watch your favorite movie for a quite small price (not available for all countries).

For reference.

As of the end of 2017, the portal database contained information on more than 4.7 million films, tv shows (including on each episode), as well as on 8.3 million film industry workers: actors, directors, screenwriters, and others.

In 1998, the famous company Amazon.com bought IMDb, which covered its entire range of entertainment services. All the information is publicly available, and just like on Wikipedia, it can be edited. IMDb has recently also been acting as a recruiting resource with social network elements for actors, screenwriters, and others involved in movie production. In order to be able to, say, offer a celebrity a role, you need to upgrade your account to IMDb pro.

Each registered user can vote on the status of a particular movie. Users form the overall rating of a film by giving it a rating from 0 to 10. Voting is absolutely free, and the project management does not affect its outcome in any way. The last section is especially interesting to us. IMDb promotion services in detail

IMDb promotion services help increase the stats of a film to improve its rating. Among these factors are positive or negative votes (one star is considered a negative rating) and the number of views of a movie.

The promotion services are done only from active IMDb accounts that have different IP addresses. These requirements are paramount. Although the admins do not influence the voting results, they still closely monitor the whole process identifying potential cases of cheating.
